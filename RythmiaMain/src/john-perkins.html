Meet John Perkins

John Perkins focuses on transformation – personal, communal, and global. His books have been on the New York Times bestseller list for more than 70 weeks, are published in over 30 languages, and have sold millions of copies.

A leading authority on shamanism, he learned about the power of using perceived reality to change objective reality – shapeshifting – from ayahuasca shamans beginning in 1968 in the Amazon rain forest – where he was initiated as shaman. After that, he studied with shamans on five continents.

He has used shamanic practices to help individuals, corporations, universities and governments. He was Chief Economist at a major international consulting firm where he advised the World

Bank, United Nations, Fortune 500 corporations, and the US and other governments. He has lectured at Harvard, Oxford and more than 50 other universities; he has been featured on ABC, NBC, CNN, CNBC, NPR, A&E, the History Channel, and in Time, The New York Times, The Washington Post, Cosmopolitan, Elle, Der Spiegel, and many other publications.

In addition to his Confessions of an Economic Hit Man and other nonfiction books on global intrigue, he has authored five books on indigenous cultures and transformation, including Shapeshifting.

He is a founder of Dream Change and (along with Bill and Lynne Twist) the Pachamama Alliance, nonprofits dedicated to creating a regenerative, peaceful, and thriving world. He was awarded the Lennon Ono Grant for Peace, 2012.
