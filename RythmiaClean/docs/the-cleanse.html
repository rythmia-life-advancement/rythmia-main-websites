    What's the colon?

  • The colon is the final part of the digestive tract extending from the cecum to the anus. It is about 6 feet long and a healthy colon has an average diameter of 2.5 inches. The inner lining of the colon is equipped with sensitive nerves and glands that aid in the assimilation of food, especially vitamins, minerals and water. These glands also help to eliminate body waste from the system. In the presence of healthy intestinal bacteria, certain vitamins are made and then absorbed. The colon has been referred to as the sewer system of the body. It is the place where we store the waste material that we would rather not think about. Most of us are not concerned with our health especially our colon until it becomes poor or we feel constipated or have diarrhea.
